Apply Nitrogen Stabilizers: Use nitrogen stabilizers or inhibitors to slow down the conversion of nitrogen into forms that are prone to leaching or volatilization. These products help increase nitrogen efficiency and reduce nutrient losses from the soil.

Implement Split Applications: Split nitrogen applications into multiple smaller doses throughout the growing season to match crop demand and minimize nitrogen losses. This approach reduces the risk of excess nitrogen buildup in the soil and improves nutrient use efficiency.

Promote Nitrogen Fixation: Utilize nitrogen-fixing cover crops or legumes in crop rotations to naturally replenish soil nitrogen levels and reduce the need for external nitrogen inputs. Incorporating nitrogen-fixing plants helps maintain soil fertility and balance nitrogen levels over time.
